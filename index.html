Disaster Preparedness in Japan

Japan is prone to natural disasters such as earthquakes, typhoons, and tsunamis. It's important for all residents to be prepared and informed.

  • Recommended Apps: Yurekuru Call (earthquake alerts), NHK News (official news), Safety Tips (multi-language disaster info).
  • Emergency Kit: Include water, food, flashlight, battery-powered radio, medications, first-aid items, passport, and cash.
  • Know Your Area: Locate your nearest evacuation shelters and check the hazard map from your local city hall.
  • Monthly Drills: Some communities and schools conduct disaster drills â€” participation is encouraged.

Smoking Rules in Public

Japan has strict laws regarding public smoking to ensure public health and cleanliness.

  • Designated Areas: Only smoke in clearly marked smoking zones â€” these are usually near train stations or public parks.
  • No Street Smoking: Walking while smoking is prohibited in many cities such as Tokyo and Kyoto.
  • Penalties: Fines for smoking in non-smoking zones can range from Â¥2,000â€“Â¥5,000.
  • Vape Rules: E-cigarettes and vapes follow the same restrictions as regular cigarettes.

Community Etiquette

Being part of a Japanese community means respecting shared spaces, participating in activities, and maintaining harmony.

  • Greetings: A simple "Konnichiwa" (hello) to your neighbors can foster good relationships.
  • Noise Awareness: Keep noise levels down, especially after 9 PM. Thin walls in apartments are common.
  • Join Local Events: Many communities host festivals (matsuri), clean-up days, and seasonal gatherings. It's a great way to meet people.
  • Garbage Sorting: Proper trash separation and collection day awareness shows respect for your community.
